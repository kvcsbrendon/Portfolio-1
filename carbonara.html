Noodles Carbonara

A classic Italian masterpiece, reimagined with a rich and creamy twist. Forget eggs and Pecorino Romano—this version features a luxurious heavy cream sauce and the smoky crunch of pre-packaged bacon bits. Perfectly paired with spaghetti cooked to a satisfyingly soft consistency, this dish brings comfort and indulgence to every bite.

Preparation Time

10 Minutes

Make Time

20 Minutes

Serving Size

2-4 Servings

Pasta Creamy Bacon Comfort Food

Instructions

Step 1 - Cook the Spaghetti

Bring a large pot of salted water to a rolling boil. Add the spaghetti and cook for 3-5 minutes longer than al dente, ensuring a soft, almost melt-in-your-mouth texture. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking. Once done, drain the pasta and set it aside, leaving a small amount of pasta water in case you need to loosen the sauce later.

Step 2 - Prepare the Cream Sauce

In a large pan over medium heat, pour in 1 cup of heavy cream and let it warm up. Stir occasionally to prevent scorching. For extra richness, add a small knob of butter and let it melt into the sauce.

Step 3 - Add the Bacon Bits

Using the back of a spoon or a pastry brush, evenly spread ½ cup of ketchup over the surface of the dough, leaving about a ½-inch border around the edges for the crust. Make sure the layer is thin but fully covers the center to avoid a dry bite.

Step 4 - Combine the Pasta and Sauce

Add the drained spaghetti directly into the pan with the cream sauce. Using tongs, toss the pasta thoroughly to coat every strand in the rich, velvety sauce. If the sauce is too thick, add a splash of reserved pasta water to achieve a smooth consistency.

Step 5 - Finish and Serve

For a final touch, sprinkle in grated Parmesan cheese and give the pasta one last toss. Serve immediately, optionally garnishing with more bacon bits and black pepper for extra flavor.

Ingredients

250g spaghetti

1 cup heavy cream

½ cup pre-packaged bacon bits

2 tbsp butter (optional)

½ cup grated Parmesan cheese

½ tsp black pepper

½ tsp garlic powder (optional)

Salt, to taste

Tools

Large pot for boiling pasta

Strainer for draining spaghetti

Large pan for cooking sauce

Tongs for tossing pasta

Grater (if using fresh Parmesan)
